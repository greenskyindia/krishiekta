Sugar jumps further on seasonal demand

Our Correspondent
Share  ·   Comment   ·   print   ·  
Tweet
Mumbai, March 27:  

Sugar prices increased further by ₹20-30 a quintal on the Vashi wholesale market on Thursday narrowing gap between cost of purchase from mills and market price which till now rules below parity. Naka rates jumped by ₹30-50 tracking firm sentiment at upper level. Mill tender rates were higher by ₹25-30 for S-grade and by ₹10 for M-grade. Undercurrent remained firm despite routine volume, said sources.

Analysts said, “Even after continuous rise in price the cost of fresh purchases remain higher for stockists as producers hold back selling lower price. Secondly end of crushing season soon across the country, less than initially expected sugar production, summer season and steady gain in world market on back of lower production in Brazil thus making possible room for Indian shipment are the key factors for lifting sugar prices.”

Arrivals at Vashi market were about 63- 64 truck loads (of 100 bags each) and the local dispatches were 60- 61 truck loads. Inventory at Vashi market is around 110-120 truck loads. On Wednesday evening, 17-18 mills offered tenders and sold about 73,000-75,000 bags at ₹2,860-2,950 (₹2,830-2,950) for S-grade and ₹2,990-3,075 (₹2,980-3,070) for M-grade.

Bombay Sugar Merchants Association's spot rates were: S-grade ₹2,982-3,072 (₹2,950-3,050) and M-grade was ₹3,092-3,182 (₹3,062-3,162). Naka delivery rates were: S-grade ₹2,960-3,030 (₹2,920-3,000) and M-grade ₹3,050-3,160 (₹3,000-3,130).

(This article was published on March 27, 2014)
